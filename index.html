About

Born and raised in Kenya, Nyagaki is an engineer and a tech educator.

She is the founder of TinkCraft, an initiative that promotes fun, creative, hands-on learning in children through STEM-themed workshops.

Prior to this, she worked at iHub Research as a Hardware Engineer, running programs such as the Kids’ Hacker Camp and Builders in Residence. Both these programs aimed at bringing out the learning potential in the youth to help them in coming up with hardware solutions to some of Africa’s challenges.

Nyagaki holds a Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Electronics Engineering degree from the University of Nairobi and has over 8 years experience in the Kenyan educational-technology (EdTech) space.

Her passion lies in developing programs that promote Research and Development within the EdTech sector thus positively influencing/complementing the Kenyan education system.

She loves experiencing the learning process in kids and young adults and doubles as a swimming instructor.

TinkCraft

Role: Founder & Program Lead

Founded in 2018, TinkCraft is an initiative that aims to nurture creativity and exploration in children. This is achieved through hands-on workshops and DIY experiments that teach basic concepts of Engineering and Design.

Workshop participants learn basic electronics and how to use kits such as Makey Makey, mBot, LittleBits and Arduino for their projects. Scratch is used as the basic programming language. As participants become more confident in coding, they move on to more advanced programming languages such as Python and C++.

TinkCraft aims to bridge the gap between the digital and physical worlds by enabling children to create seamlessly between both worlds.

Builders-in-Residence Program

Role: Project Manager - BuildLab

Designed and run to support local, young and enterprising hardware innovators, The Builders-in-REsidence Program (BIR) grew from a notable gap in the development of hands-on hardware entrepreneurship skills in Kenyan tertiary institutions. I led this program as an employee of the iHub.

BIR served to give local hardware innovators the tools and skills neeed to transform their solutions from initial prototypes to market-ready products. It entailed a three to six-month training during which the “builders” had access to a co-working space to collaboratively build their hardware solutions. They also had access to Market Research, User Experience Design, Business Strategy and tools for early phase development of their solutions.

BIR partnered with companies who wanted solutions from their hardware bootcamps and competitions developed. This included companies such as Intel and Lions of Science which signed up their winning teams to the BIR program.

KIDS' HACKER CAMP

Role: Coordinator

Kids Hacker Camp(KHC) is a week-long camp hosted at iHub_ – Nairobi’s Innovation Hub for the technology community. The camp is aimed at kids and teens aged 7 – 18 years, who are drawn to and interested in crafting, hacking, discovering and tinkering. The camp is envisioned and designed to provide an engaging platform on which children and teens can interactively learn, explore and build; spurring them to understand the world around them, analogue and digital. This exposure and tutelage then goes on to help them build skills in design/creativity, critical & systems thinking, collaboration and self-confidence. Watch a video of the program here

Paricipants of KHC had the option to enrol in the Waza Experience. Waza (Swahili for “Active Thinking”) Experience aimed to instill creativity, problem solving, and communication by guiding students through a curriculum based on Design Thinking. Through Waza, we hoped to open up minds and spur creative solutions to everyday problems

Over 100 stdents graduated from the KHC program and more than 50 trainers worked with us over the span of two years. Both participants and trainers gained a lot in terms of hardware knowledge and soft skills.

Fab Lab Robotics Program

Role: Trainer

The FabLab Robotics Outreach Program (FROP) is an interactive kids electronic maker program in Kenya that aims at making Science, Technology, Engineering and Maths (STEM) fun and practical. The program aims to improve the African child’s chances at flourishing in STEM careers.

I was privileged to undertake an Engineering degree at one of Kenya’s leading Universities. Having the chance to share my knowledge with underprivileged children and inspire them was a great way to give back. Our team worked with children living in in Kibera, Baba Dogo & Dagoretti slum areas in Kenya, with a particular focus on girls. Our goal was to inspire the next generation of scientists, researchers, and innovators in Africa.

We were able to partner with The Girl Effect (Nike Foundation), Kuweni Serious, FunKidz Kenya and the Safaricom Women in Technology program. This expanded the reach of our work to over 500 students in five years and sustained the program financially.

Most notably, we partnered with Kuweni Serious and the Nike Foundation to run the 125/100 program. Through this program, we taught robotics, engineering, citizen journalism and design to 125 girls in 100 days in Kenya slum areas. Watch the video here
